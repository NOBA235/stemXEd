PHYSICS FORMULA REFERENCE SHEET
For use with NCERT, CBSE, IGCSE, AP and IB curricula

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
MECHANICS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

KINEMATICS (constant acceleration)
  v = u + at
  s = ut + ½at²
  v² = u² + 2as
  s = (u + v)/2 × t
  Where: u = initial velocity, v = final velocity, a = acceleration, t = time, s = displacement

FREE FALL
  g = 9.8 m/s² (use 10 m/s² for quick estimates)
  For object dropped from rest: v = gt, h = ½gt²
  Time to fall height h: t = √(2h/g)

NEWTON'S LAWS
  First Law:  An object stays at rest or constant velocity unless acted on by a net force
  Second Law: F = ma  (Net force = mass × acceleration)
  Third Law:  For every action there is an equal and opposite reaction

WEIGHT AND MASS
  W = mg  (Weight = mass × gravitational field strength)
  On Earth: g = 9.8 N/kg ≈ 10 N/kg

FRICTION
  f = μN  (friction force = coefficient of friction × normal force)
  Static friction: f_s ≤ μ_s × N
  Kinetic friction: f_k = μ_k × N

MOMENTUM AND IMPULSE
  p = mv  (momentum = mass × velocity)
  Impulse = F × t = change in momentum = m(v - u)
  Conservation of momentum: m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂

WORK, ENERGY AND POWER
  Work = F × d × cos θ  (force × distance × cosine of angle between them)
  Kinetic Energy: KE = ½mv²
  Gravitational Potential Energy: PE = mgh
  Elastic Potential Energy: PE = ½kx²
  Conservation of energy: Total energy is constant in a closed system
  Power: P = W/t = F × v (watts)
  Efficiency = (useful output energy / total input energy) × 100%

CIRCULAR MOTION
  Centripetal acceleration: a = v²/r = ω²r
  Centripetal force: F = mv²/r = mω²r
  Angular velocity: ω = 2π/T = 2πf
  Period and frequency: T = 1/f

GRAVITATION
  Newton's Law: F = Gm₁m₂/r²  (G = 6.67 × 10⁻¹¹ N m² kg⁻²)
  Gravitational field strength: g = GM/r²
  Orbital speed: v = √(GM/r)
  Escape velocity: v = √(2GM/r)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
WAVES AND SOUND
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Wave speed: v = fλ  (speed = frequency × wavelength)
  Speed of sound in air: ≈ 340 m/s at room temperature
  Speed of light: c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s
  Period and frequency: T = 1/f

DOPPLER EFFECT
  Observed frequency when source moves toward observer: f' = f × v/(v - v_s)
  Observed frequency when source moves away: f' = f × v/(v + v_s)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ELECTRICITY AND MAGNETISM
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

OHM'S LAW
  V = IR  (Voltage = Current × Resistance)
  I = V/R
  R = V/I

ELECTRICAL POWER
  P = IV = I²R = V²/R  (Power = current × voltage)
  Energy = Power × time = IVt (joules)

SERIES CIRCUITS
  Total resistance: R_total = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ + ...
  Same current through all components
  Voltages add up: V_total = V₁ + V₂ + V₃ + ...

PARALLEL CIRCUITS
  1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃ + ...
  Same voltage across all components
  Currents add up: I_total = I₁ + I₂ + I₃ + ...

CAPACITORS
  Q = CV  (charge = capacitance × voltage)
  Energy stored: E = ½CV² = Q²/2C
  Series: 1/C_total = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂
  Parallel: C_total = C₁ + C₂

MAGNETIC FORCE
  Force on current: F = BIL sin θ
  Force on charge: F = qvB sin θ
  Magnetic flux: Φ = BA cos θ
  EMF induced: ε = -N × ΔΦ/Δt  (Faraday's Law)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
THERMODYNAMICS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Heat: Q = mcΔT  (heat = mass × specific heat capacity × temperature change)
  Specific latent heat: Q = mL
  Ideal gas law: PV = nRT  (R = 8.314 J mol⁻¹ K⁻¹)
  Boyle's Law: P₁V₁ = P₂V₂  (constant temperature)
  Charles's Law: V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂  (constant pressure)
  First Law of Thermodynamics: ΔU = Q - W
  Efficiency of heat engine: η = 1 - T_cold/T_hot

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
OPTICS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Snell's Law: n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂
  Refractive index: n = c/v = sin i / sin r
  Critical angle: sin C = 1/n  (for total internal reflection)
  Mirror/lens equation: 1/f = 1/v + 1/u
  Magnification: m = v/u = image height / object height

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
MODERN PHYSICS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Photoelectric effect: E = hf = hc/λ
  Planck's constant: h = 6.63 × 10⁻³⁴ J s
  de Broglie wavelength: λ = h/mv = h/p
  Einstein's mass-energy: E = mc²
  Radioactive decay: N = N₀ × (½)^(t/t½)
