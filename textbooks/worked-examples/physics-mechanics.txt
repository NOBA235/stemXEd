EXAMPLE 1: Object dropped from rest
Topic: Free fall, kinematics
Difficulty: Easy

PROBLEM: A stone is dropped from the top of a building 45m tall.
How long does it take to reach the ground? (g = 10 m/s²)

SOLUTION:
Given:
  Initial velocity u = 0 (dropped from rest, not thrown)
  Distance s = 45 m
  Acceleration a = g = 10 m/s²

We need: time t

Using s = ut + ½at²
  45 = 0 × t + ½ × 10 × t²
  45 = 5t²
  t² = 45/5 = 9
  t = 3 seconds

ANSWER: The stone takes 3 seconds to reach the ground.

Key insight: When an object starts from rest (u = 0), the equation simplifies to s = ½at².
The initial velocity term disappears, making the calculation much simpler.

EXAMPLE 2: Object thrown upward
Topic: Vertical projectile motion
Difficulty: Medium

PROBLEM: A ball is thrown vertically upward with velocity 20 m/s.
Find: (a) maximum height, (b) time to reach maximum height, (c) total time of flight

SOLUTION:
Given: u = 20 m/s (upward), g = 10 m/s² (downward, so a = -10 m/s²)

(a) Maximum height — at maximum height, velocity v = 0
Using v² = u² + 2as:
  0 = 20² + 2 × (-10) × s
  0 = 400 - 20s
  s = 400/20 = 20 m

(b) Time to maximum height
Using v = u + at:
  0 = 20 + (-10) × t
  10t = 20
  t = 2 seconds

(c) Total flight time
By symmetry, time going up = time coming down
Total time = 2 × 2 = 4 seconds

ANSWER: Maximum height = 20 m, time up = 2 s, total flight = 4 s.

Key insight: At maximum height, velocity = 0. Use this as your "final" condition.
The flight is symmetric — time up equals time down.

EXAMPLE 3: Horizontal projectile
Topic: Projectile motion
Difficulty: Medium

PROBLEM: A ball rolls off a table 1.25m high with horizontal velocity 4 m/s.
How far from the base of the table does it land? (g = 10 m/s²)

SOLUTION:
Horizontal and vertical motion are INDEPENDENT.

Vertical (finding time to fall):
  s = ut + ½gt²
  1.25 = 0 + ½ × 10 × t²
  1.25 = 5t²
  t² = 0.25
  t = 0.5 s

Horizontal (finding range):
  x = horizontal velocity × time
  x = 4 × 0.5 = 2 m

ANSWER: The ball lands 2 m from the base of the table.

Key insight: Treat horizontal and vertical as completely separate problems.
Find the time from the vertical motion, then use it for the horizontal calculation.

EXAMPLE 4: Basic F = ma
Topic: Newton's Second Law
Difficulty: Easy

PROBLEM: A 1200 kg car accelerates from 0 to 30 m/s in 10 seconds.
What net force acts on the car?

SOLUTION:
First find acceleration:
  a = (v - u) / t = (30 - 0) / 10 = 3 m/s²

Then find force:
  F = ma = 1200 × 3 = 3600 N

ANSWER: The net force on the car is 3600 N.

EXAMPLE 5: Block on frictionless surface
Topic: Newton's Second Law, frictionless
Difficulty: Easy

PROBLEM: A 5 kg block is pushed with a 20 N force on a frictionless surface.
Find the acceleration.

SOLUTION:
Since the surface is frictionless, the net force equals the applied force.
  F_net = 20 N, m = 5 kg

Using F = ma:
  a = F/m = 20/5 = 4 m/s²

ANSWER: The acceleration is 4 m/s².

Key insight: On a frictionless surface, ALL the applied force goes into acceleration.
No force is "wasted" overcoming friction.

EXAMPLE 6: Block on surface WITH friction
Topic: Newton's Second Law, friction
Difficulty: Medium

PROBLEM: A 10 kg block is pushed with 40 N force on a surface with μ = 0.2.
Find the acceleration. (g = 10 m/s²)

SOLUTION:
Step 1: Find normal force
  On flat surface: N = mg = 10 × 10 = 100 N

Step 2: Find friction force
  f = μN = 0.2 × 100 = 20 N

Step 3: Find net force
  F_net = Applied force - Friction = 40 - 20 = 20 N

Step 4: Find acceleration
  a = F_net / m = 20 / 10 = 2 m/s²

ANSWER: The acceleration is 2 m/s².

Key insight: The net force is what causes acceleration, not the applied force alone.
Always subtract friction before applying F = ma.

EXAMPLE 7: Conservation of momentum (collision)
Topic: Momentum, conservation laws
Difficulty: Medium

PROBLEM: A 2 kg trolley moving at 6 m/s collides with a stationary 4 kg trolley.
They stick together. Find their common velocity after collision.

SOLUTION:
Conservation of momentum: total momentum before = total momentum after

Before collision:
  p₁ = m₁u₁ = 2 × 6 = 12 kg m/s
  p₂ = m₂u₂ = 4 × 0 = 0

After collision (they stick, so same velocity v):
  p_total = (m₁ + m₂) × v = (2 + 4) × v = 6v

Setting equal:
  6v = 12 + 0 = 12
  v = 2 m/s

ANSWER: They move together at 2 m/s.

Key insight: When objects stick together (perfectly inelastic collision), 
treat them as one combined mass after the collision.

EXAMPLE 8: Energy conversion (roller coaster)
Topic: Conservation of energy, KE and PE
Difficulty: Medium

PROBLEM: A 500 kg roller coaster car starts from rest at the top of a 30 m hill.
What is its speed at the bottom? (ignore friction, g = 10 m/s²)

SOLUTION:
By conservation of energy: PE at top = KE at bottom

PE at top = mgh = 500 × 10 × 30 = 150,000 J

KE at bottom = ½mv²
  150,000 = ½ × 500 × v²
  150,000 = 250v²
  v² = 600
  v = √600 ≈ 24.5 m/s

ANSWER: The speed at the bottom is approximately 24.5 m/s.

Key insight: All the potential energy converts to kinetic energy (when friction is ignored).
This is conservation of energy — energy changes form but the total stays the same.
